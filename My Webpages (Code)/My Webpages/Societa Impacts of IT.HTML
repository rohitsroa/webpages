ComputerVirus

  • A Virus is a small self replicating software program that infects a computer without the permission or knowledge of the owner.
  • Virus stands for Vital Information Resource Under Siege.
  • A Virus can affect another program, appication, computer boot sector or document. This way it spreads from one computer to another and to interfere with the computer operation.
  • Viruses spread easily through attachments to an e-mail message or a downloaded file. It may be present on a CD or a Pen Drive.
  • They Cause damage like corrupting or deleting data on your computer, use your e-mail program to spread itself to other computers, or even erase everything on your hard disk.

Types Of ComputerVirus

  • Boot Sector Virus
    • This Type of Virus attacks the boot sector by attaching itself to the DOS Boot sector on floppy diskettes or the master boot record on the Hard Disk.
    • They are also called as the boot record infector or System Virus. Such Viruses are of the Memory Resident Type.
  • File Virus
    • File Viruses attack and modify the executable or program files (having extension .exe, .com, .bat).
    • Normally they load themselves on to the memory and slowly infect other files.
